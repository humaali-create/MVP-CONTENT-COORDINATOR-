# Content Navigator

## Product Overview

**Content Navigator** is a catalog assistant tool designed to help Content Coordinators quickly find answers about their streaming content library. Instead of spending hours searching through thousands of titles and creating reports manually, users can ask questions about their catalog and receive clear summaries that help them understand trends, gaps, and available content.

**Created by:** Huma Ali

---

## Problem Statement

The Content Coordinator spends too much time searching through thousands of titles and making reports because the catalog information is not easy to use for quick answers. This makes it harder and slower for her to prepare meetings and help the team make content decisions.

---

## Solution Description

Content Navigator helps Content Coordinators quickly get answers about their streaming catalog without spending hours searching through data. Users can ask simple questions about their content, and the tool provides clear summaries about trends, gaps, and what is available in their library. This saves time and helps teams make faster, better decisions.

---

## MVP User Flow

The MVP focuses on helping users ask questions about their content catalog and receive useful summaries.

| Step | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | User opens the catalog assistant tool. |
| 2 | User enters a question about the content catalog (example: "How many international movies were added this year?"). |
| 3 | The tool searches the content catalog data. |
| 4 | The tool analyzes the information and creates a simple summary. |
| 5 | User reviews the answer and uses the information for meetings or decisions. |
| 6 | User asks another question or updates the search if they need more details. |

---

## Target User

**Primary User:** Content Coordinator

Content Coordinators use Content Navigator to quickly understand their streaming catalog, prepare reports, and support content planning decisions.

---

## Key Benefits

- Reduces time spent manually searching through large content catalogs.
- Provides quick answers to catalog questions.
- Helps users identify trends and gaps in available content.
- Makes meeting preparation and decision-making easier.
